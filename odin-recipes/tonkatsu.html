Tonkatsu

My favorite childhood Japanese Food. Great fry.

Ingredients:

  • Pork loin or pork tenderloin fillet
  • Salt and black pepper
  • All-purpose flour
  • Large eggs
  • Panko breadcrumbs
  • Any neutral flavored oil, such as vegetable oil
  • Tonkatsu sauce
  • Black and white sesame seeds

Steps:

  1. Step 1 – Make the dipping sauce. Grind the sesame seeds in a mortar and pestle. Divide the powder into small sauce bowls and top with tonkatsu sauce.
  2. Step 2 – Prepare the pork. Trim the excess fat and cut slits into the connective tissue. Pound the pork with a meat mallet, then season with salt and pepper.
  3. Step 3—Set up the breading station. Arrange three shallow trays with flour, beaten eggs whisked with oil, and panko. Prepare a deep pot with oil and heat on medium to 340ºF (170ºC).
  4. Step 4 – Bread the pork. Dredge the cutlets in flour, egg mixture, and panko.
  5. Step 5 – Fry. Cook one cutlet in the hot oil for 1 minute, then flip and cook for another minute. Transfer to a wire rack and fry the other cutlets.
